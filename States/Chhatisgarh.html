Traditional Dance Forms of Chhattisgarh

Celebrating the rich tribal heritage and folk rhythms of Chhattisgarh

Panthi

Panthi is a spiritual dance performed by the Satnami community. It involves soulful songs and expressive movements reflecting devotion and philosophical teachings of Guru Ghasidas.

Raut Nacha

Performed during Deepawali by Yadavs, Raut Nacha is a traditional dance celebrating the cowherd heritage and the stories of Lord Krishna. It includes sticks, vibrant costumes, and rhythmic steps.

Sua Nacha

Sua Nacha or Parrot Dance is performed by women, usually during harvest. It symbolizes fertility and is accompanied by clapping, singing, and a symbolic parrot in the center.

Karma

Karma dance is performed by tribal communities like Gonds and Oraons. It celebrates the Karma tree and features circular formations, singing, and energetic footwork.

Gendi

In Gendi dance, performers use bamboo stilts and display incredible balance and coordination. It is performed during festivals and village fairs, especially by children and youth.

Dandari

Dandari is a festive dance representing mythical stories, usually performed during post-harvest celebrations. Dancers hold sticks and form various symbolic formations.
