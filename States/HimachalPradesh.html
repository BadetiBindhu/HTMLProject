Traditional Dance Forms of Himachal Pradesh

Explore the rich folk traditions and highland rhythms of Himachal's vibrant communities

Nati

Nati is the most popular folk dance of Himachal Pradesh, especially in Kullu, Sirmaur, and Shimla regions. It is performed in circular formations with rhythmic steps and traditional music.

Chhanak Chham

Chhanak Chham is a vibrant masked dance performed by the Buddhist Lamas in monasteries during religious festivals. It is a spiritual dance with elaborate costumes and dramatic movements.

Kayang Mala

Kayang Mala is performed by men and women who form a human chain. The dance is slow and graceful, symbolizing harmony and unity among communities.

Dangi

Dangi is a harvest dance of the Chamba region. It is performed in honor of deities and includes slow rhythmic movements in traditional attire with colorful scarves.

Losar Shona Chuksam

Performed during the Tibetan New Year (Losar), this dance involves vibrant masks and traditional dresses, symbolizing the cleansing of evil spirits and welcoming prosperity.

Shand and Shabu

These are traditional wedding dances of the Lahaul region, performed with melodious folk songs and slow-paced coordinated steps representing joy and togetherness.
