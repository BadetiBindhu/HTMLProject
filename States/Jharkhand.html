Traditional Dance Forms of Jharkhand

Experience the cultural heartbeat of Jharkhand through its tribal and folk dances

Paika

Paika is a martial folk dance performed by men wielding swords and shields. It reflects valor and tribal strength and is often showcased during festivals and tribal fairs.

Chhau

Chhau is a semi-classical masked dance blending martial arts, tribal traditions, and folk storytelling. It is widely performed in Saraikela and celebrates mythology and heroism.

Jhumar

Jhumar is a joyful folk dance performed during harvest season. With slow, graceful movements and songs of love and nature, it represents rural life and celebration.

Mundari

The Mundari dance is performed by the Munda tribe of Jharkhand. It's a community dance involving rhythmic clapping and steps, celebrating seasonal events and tribal unity.

Santhali

Santhali dance is performed by the Santhal tribe. Dancers wear colorful attire and perform to the beats of drums and flutes, often forming long chains and moving in sync.

Karma

Karma is performed during the Karma festival to honor the Karma tree. It involves both men and women dancing in circles, celebrating nature, fertility, and tribal beliefs.
