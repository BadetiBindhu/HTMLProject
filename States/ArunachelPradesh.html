Traditional Dance Forms of Arunachal Pradesh

Celebrating the vibrant culture and rhythm of the northeastern frontier

Ponung

Ponung is a traditional group dance performed by the Adi tribe of Arunachal Pradesh, usually before harvesting season. Women form a circle and sing melodious folk songs, accompanied by a lead male musician.

Aji Lamu

Aji Lamu is a masked dance-drama performed by the Monpa tribe during the Losar festival. The dance narrates the story of the birth of Lord Buddha and features vibrant costumes and elaborate storytelling.

Chalo

Chalo is a dance of the Nocte tribe, performed during festivals like Loku. It showcases war-like movements and high energy, celebrating valor, strength, and tribal unity.

Buiya

Buiya is a traditional dance of the Digaru Mishmi tribe of Arunachal Pradesh, performed during festivals like Tamladu. It features vibrant group movements and reflects joy, unity, and reverence for nature and ancestral spirits.

Aji Lamu

Aji Lamu is a masked dance-drama performed by the Monpa tribe during the Losar festival. The dance narrates the story of the birth of Lord Buddha and features vibrant costumes and elaborate storytelling.

Wancho Dance

The Wancho tribe performs this dance during the Oriah festival. It features traditional attire and rhythmic drumbeats symbolizing joy, prosperity, and unity within the tribe.

Rikhampada

Rikhampada is a folk dance of the Nishis and the Tagins of Arunachal Pradesh. It involves intricate footwork and mimics animal movements, symbolizing harmony with nature.

Lion & Peacock Dance

Performed by the Monpa tribe, this unique dance enacts the play between a lion and a peacock. It involves dramatic costumes and is staged during festive celebrations like Losar.
