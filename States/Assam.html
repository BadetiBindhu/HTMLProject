Traditional Dance Forms of Assam

Discover the rich cultural heritage and rhythm of Assam through its vibrant dance traditions

Bihu

Bihu is the most popular folk dance of Assam, performed during the Rongali Bihu festival. It is characterized by brisk dance steps and rapid hand movements, celebrating spring and fertility.

Sattriya

Sattriya is a classical dance form of Assam introduced by the saint Srimanta Sankardev in the 15th century. It is devotional in nature and performed in monasteries called Satras.

Bagurumba

Performed by the Bodo tribe, Bagurumba is a graceful dance that mimics nature and butterflies. Dancers wear colorful traditional attire and move in rhythmic harmony.

Jhumur

Jhumur is a folk dance performed by tea garden communities of Assam. It features synchronized group movements and is usually accompanied by traditional music and songs.

Bhortal

Bhortal is a vibrant dance created by Guru Narayan Bharati. It involves cymbals and is performed by male dancers in synchronized formations with fast beats and devotional music.

Deodhani

Deodhani is a ritualistic dance performed by women in a trance-like state, associated with the worship of the goddess Manasa. It is intense and spiritual in nature.
