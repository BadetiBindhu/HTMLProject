Traditional Dance Forms of Bihar

Explore the rich folk traditions and cultural expressions of Bihar through its classical and folk dances

Jat-Jatin

Jat-Jatin is a traditional duet dance from North Bihar performed by a man and woman representing a couple. It narrates stories of love, hardship, and local legends during monsoon.

Jhijhiya

Performed by women during the Dussehra festival, Jhijhiya involves balancing earthen pots with multiple holes and lamps on the head while dancing to ward off evil spirits.

Bidesia

Bidesia is a folk theatre and dance form created by Bhikhari Thakur. It deals with themes of migration, social issues, and emotional struggles through music, acting, and dance.

Sama-Chakeva

Performed during the winter months, Sama-Chakeva is associated with brother-sister bonding. Girls enact the story of Sama and her brother Chakeva using clay idols and dance.

Kajari

Kajari is a monsoon folk dance performed by women in Bihar and Uttar Pradesh. It includes emotional songs of longing and celebration, typically under the cloudy sky.
