Traditional Dance Forms of Gujarat

Celebrate the colorful spirit and rich heritage of Gujarat through its folk dances

Garba

Garba is a traditional Gujarati dance performed in a circular formation around a lamp or image of the goddess Durga, especially during Navratri. It's energetic and devotional.

Dandiya Raas

Dandiya is a festive dance using colorful sticks, performed mostly during Navratri. It represents a mock-fight between Goddess Durga and the demon Mahishasura.

Tippani

Tippani is a labor-oriented dance performed by women of Saurashtra using long wooden sticks. It originated as a rhythmic way to flatten floors and now symbolizes teamwork and festivity.

Padhar

Padhar is a folk dance of the Padhar tribe, performed near lakes or rivers. It mimics rowing, fishing, and water-related movements in a graceful storytelling style.

Bhavai

Bhavai is a dramatic dance that combines storytelling, balancing acts, and traditional music. Performers often balance multiple pots on their heads while dancing on swords or glass.

Hudo

Hudo is a dance of the Bharwad community, performed in groups with rhythmic claps and synchronized footwork. It is performed during festivals and social gatherings.
